Odell Beckham Jr.: Early Life, Family, and Education

Beckham was born on 5th November of 1992. He was born to his father, Odell Beckham Sr., and Heather Van Norman, his mother.

The Beckham Sr played football in college as a Running back(RB) at LSU from 1990-1992.

OBJ’s mother, Heather Van Norman, was a sprinter for LSU from 1989-1995. She was a six-time All-American who played for the Tigers.

Odell is the eldest child of his parents. He has a brother, Kordell Beckham, and a sister, Jasmine Beckham.

As a child, he was fascinated with sports, where he was a letterman in football, basketball, and track.

The player also was interested in soccer; his idol was David Beckham.

He played as a quarterback, wide receiver, cornerback, and running back for the Greenies football team in football.

As both his parents and his stepdad were incredible athletes, he was brought up in a sporting manner.

The wide receiver gets his thunderous self-confidence from the full support of his athletic parents.

He shone since his childhood; as a junior, he caught 45 passes for 743 yards and 10 touchdowns.

As a senior, he caught 50 catches for 1010 yards and 19 touchdowns.

He went to high school in Isidore Newman School, and he chose Louisiana State University for his college. The same college his parents went to.

Odell Beckham Jr.: Career

New York Giants

The New York Giants drafted Beckham in the 12th overall pick in the 1st round of the NFL Draft 2014.

He signed a deal for $10.4 million with the Giants and got a $5.88 million signing bonus on 19 May 2014.

On 23rd November of 2014, Beckham made a one-handed touchdown reception hailed as the “Catch of the year.”

Moreover, LeBron James calls this one of the best catches ever.

In 2015, Beckham was given the title of Offensive Rookie of the Year.

He was selected as the cover of Madden NFL 16, a video game by EA sports.

Similarly, in the 2016 season, he was ranked 8th on the NFL Top 100 Players of 2017.

He has also played with the famous Rookie Saquon Barkley.

In 2018, Beckham signed a five-year, $95 million contract extension with the Giants but left for the Cleveland Browns in March 2019.

Cleveland Browns

The Cleveland Browns acquired Beckham on 13 March 2019.

In a game against the Baltimore Ravens, Beckham punched Ravens’ cornerback Marlon Humphrey.

Who then responded by choking Beckham. They got into a fight in the middle of the match.

Further, Beckham was fined $14,037, as was Humphrey.

After the game against Seattle Seahawks, Odell was fined $14,037 by the NFL for a uniform violation.

Moreover, he wasn’t covering his ankles as it was supposed to be covered.

Beckham wore Joker-inspired cleats against the Denver Broncos.

Unfortunately, this violated the NFL’s policy on cleat colors, and he had to change cleats during half-time.

Beckham had surpassed 1,000 receiving yards against the Cincinnati Bengals with Cleveland Browns.

Beckham was battling with a sports hernia. He underwent core-muscle surgery on 21 January.

Beckham suffered a torn ACL against Cincinnati Bengals, ending his season.

Beckham finished the whole of 2020 with 23 catches for 319 yards.

He also had three receiving touchdowns plus three rushes for 72 yards and a rushing touchdown in only a minimum of seven games played.
