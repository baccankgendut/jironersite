Early Life

Matt Ryan was born on 17th May 1985. His full name is Matthew Thomas Ryan. His parents Bernice and Michael Ryan, have Irish ancestry and are Roman Catholics. Ryan is the third of his four siblings. Ryan hd his schooling at the William Penn Charter School in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania.

When he was in high school, he played football, basketball, and baseball and was the captain of all three teams. He was a fan of the Philadelphia Eagles. He earned All-East honors from Larunt Lemming Prep Football Report.

Ryan started getting athletic scholarship offers from Purdue, Georgia Tech, Temple, and Connecticut. Later he chose Boston College as he was looking for a university closer to home.

Career

In 2003 Ryan was in Boston College and in the 2004 season, made his collegiate debut as a quarterback and played till 2006. In 2007 Ryan led his team to an ACC Atlantic Division championship in which he was awarded as ACC Player of the Year.

In the 2008 NFL Draft, Ryan was drafted by the Atlanta Falcons in the first round. He signed a six-year contract, worth a stupendous $72 million! This is one of the highest of NFL contracts. Yet Ryan was offered this contract without ever having played a single professional game.

In the 2008 season, Ryan became the first rookie quarterback to start a season for the Falcons, and for his brilliant performance, earned the title of Diet Pepsi Rookie of the Week. Ryan also became the first Falcon player to be awarded the NFC Rookie of the Month for October 2008. He was also named NFC Offensive Player of the Week.

In 2009 the Falcons had two consecutive winning seasons with 9-7 for the first time in franchise history. In the 2010 season, Ryan led the Falcons to their best record of 13–3. Ryan was invited to the 2011 Pro Bowl, for the first time of his career.

In the 2011 season, Ryan led the Falcons to a 10–6 record, and in 2012 season, Ryan led the Falcons to their franchise-best start of the season, starting 8–0.

In 2013, Ryan had a five-year extension if the contract, valued at $103.75 million with the Falcons. This was a bad year for Falcons with a 4–12 record. In 2014 season, Ryan led the Falcons to a 6–10, and in 2015 season to an 8–8 record. These three seasons, the team could not qualify for the playoff.

In the 2016 season, Ryan was selected to his fourth Pro Bowl and was named First-team All-Pro. Ryan was recognized as the NFL Offensive Player of the Year and the NFL Most Valuable Player for the 2016 season. He was also ranked tenth on the NFL Top 100 Players.
